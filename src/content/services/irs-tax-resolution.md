## What's Included

Resolving a tax problem almost never comes down to a single form or phone call. The process typically involves several moving parts, and we handle them together:

**Back taxes and unfiled returns.** Before anything else can be resolved, the IRS needs to see filed returns. We obtain your account transcript, identify every missing year, gather the records, prepare the returns, and file them correctly.

**IRS and Georgia DOR notice response.** Whether the notice involves a proposed assessment, a balance due, a request for documentation, or something else, we review it, explain what it means in plain English, and respond formally on your behalf.

**Penalty and payment-plan guidance.** Once returns are filed and balances are established, there are usually options: installment agreements, currently-not-collectible status, penalty abatement requests where circumstances support them. We explain the realistic possibilities and help you pursue the arrangements that fit your situation — without overpromising results.

**Representation as your CPA.** You don't have to speak directly with the IRS or Georgia DOR. We can communicate on your behalf, which removes a significant source of stress and reduces the risk of saying something unintentionally harmful to your case.

**Getting — and staying — compliant.** Resolving the backlog matters; so does making sure the same situation doesn't develop again. We'll make sure your future filing and payment obligations are clear before we're done.

## How the Process Works

Every engagement starts with an honest assessment. We pull your IRS transcript, review any notices you've received, and take stock of what years are open, what's owed, and what options the timeline leaves available.

From there, we work through the missing pieces: preparing and filing any delinquent returns, calculating what's owed, and identifying the most appropriate path forward with the taxing authority. If a payment arrangement makes sense, we handle the paperwork and negotiation. If a penalty abatement request is warranted, we prepare it. We keep you informed at each stage so there are no surprises.

Once the immediate problem is resolved, we review your going-forward obligations — quarterly estimates, withholding, filing deadlines — so you leave with a clear picture, not just a closed file.
