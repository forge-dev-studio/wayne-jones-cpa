## Bookkeeping Services in Rome, GA for Small Businesses and Individuals

Running a small business in Rome or the surrounding Floyd County area means wearing a lot of hats. Managing your own books on top of everything else is one hat too many for most business owners — and when it does not get done consistently, the consequences show up at tax time in the form of scrambled records, missed deductions, and avoidable stress.

Wayne Jones Accounting Services provides monthly bookkeeping and write-up services for small businesses and individuals throughout Rome, NW Georgia, and NE Alabama. Our clients get accurate, organized financials every month — handled by a licensed CPA, not a data-entry service.

## What Bookkeeping with Wayne Jones Includes

Our monthly bookkeeping service covers the core tasks that keep your financial picture accurate and current.

Each month, we reconcile your bank and credit card accounts, ensuring your records match your actual statements. We maintain your general ledger and produce profit-and-loss statements and other financial reports that show you where your business actually stands. Sales-tax tracking is included so your liability is calculated correctly throughout the year rather than estimated at filing time. And because we keep your books continuously — not just at year-end — your records are already organized when it is time to prepare your tax return.

This is not a one-size-fits-all data dump. It is structured monthly work designed around the needs of small businesses operating in Georgia.

## How the Monthly Bookkeeping Process Works

At the start of each month, we receive or access the prior month's transaction data — bank statements, credit card activity, and any supporting records. We reconcile accounts, categorize transactions, and update the ledger. If anything looks unusual or needs your input, we will reach out. At the end of the process, you have clean financials for the month.

You work directly with Wayne, not a rotating staff of bookkeepers. That consistency matters. Wayne knows your accounts, your business, and your history. When something changes — a new vendor, a large one-time expense, a shift in sales volume — it gets noticed and handled correctly.

The other practical benefit is what happens at tax time. When your books are current and accurate through December 31, there is no reconstruction phase. We move directly into preparation. That is a real advantage for businesses that want their returns done efficiently and correctly.
