Getting ready for your tax appointment doesn't have to be stressful. A little prep work upfront saves time in the office and helps make sure nothing gets missed. This checklist covers the most common documents and information clients bring to their appointments at Wayne Jones Accounting Services in Rome, GA.

Not every item on this list applies to everyone. If you're unsure what's relevant to your situation, just call us at **(706) 232-8565** — we're happy to walk you through it before you come in.

---

## Personal Identification

Before anything else, bring a way to verify your identity and the identities of everyone on your return.

- Government-issued photo ID (driver's license, state ID, or passport)
- Social Security numbers (SSNs) or Individual Taxpayer Identification Numbers (ITINs) for yourself, your spouse, and any dependents
- Dates of birth for all family members on the return

---

## Income Documents

This is the core of most returns. Gather everything that shows money you received during the year.

### Wages and Salary
- **W-2** from each employer (you should receive one per job)

### Self-Employment and Freelance Income
- **1099-NEC** (nonemployee compensation from clients or platforms)
- **1099-K** (payment card or third-party network transactions, such as PayPal or Venmo business accounts)
- Records of any cash income not covered by a 1099

### Investment and Financial Income
- **1099-INT** (bank and savings interest)
- **1099-DIV** (dividends)
- **1099-B** (proceeds from stock, mutual fund, or securities sales)
- **1099-S** (proceeds from real estate sales)
- Records of cost basis for any investments sold

### Retirement and Government Benefits
- **1099-R** (distributions from pensions, IRAs, annuities, or 401(k) plans)
- **SSA-1099** (Social Security benefits)

### Other Income Sources
- **Schedule K-1** (income from a partnership, S-corporation, estate, or trust)
- Rental income and related expense records
- Alimony received (for agreements entered before 2019)
- Gambling winnings (W-2G, if issued)
- Any other income not listed above

## Deductions and Credits

These are the items that can reduce what you owe. Bring whatever documentation applies to your situation.

### Home and Real Estate
- **1098** (mortgage interest statement from your lender)
- Property tax payment records
- Closing documents if you bought or sold a home during the year

### Charitable Contributions
- Receipts or acknowledgment letters for cash donations
- Records of noncash donations (clothing, household goods, vehicle) with estimated fair market values
- Mileage logs if you drove for charitable purposes

### Medical Expenses
- Receipts or statements for out-of-pocket medical, dental, and vision costs
- Records of health insurance premiums paid (especially if self-employed)
- Health Savings Account (HSA) contributions and distributions — look for **Form 5498-SA** and **1099-SA**

### Education
- **1098-T** (tuition statement from a college or university)
- Records of student loan interest paid (**1098-E**)
- Receipts for eligible education expenses

### Childcare and Dependent Care
- Name, address, and Tax ID (or SSN) of your childcare provider
- Total amount paid for childcare during the year

### Energy and Home Improvements
- Records and receipts for qualifying energy-efficient home improvements (solar panels, heat pumps, insulation, etc.)
- Manufacturer certifications, if available

## If You Own a Business

Small business owners typically need more documentation. Bring as much of the following as you have organized.

### Income and Revenue
- Profit and loss summary for the year (or your full bookkeeping records)
- Bank statements for business accounts
- **1099s received** from clients or platforms
- Records of any other business income

### Expenses
- Receipts or records for major business expenses: supplies, equipment, software, professional services, advertising, insurance, etc.
- **Mileage log** — total business miles driven, with dates and purposes
- **Home office records** — square footage of dedicated office space and total home square footage, plus home expense totals (mortgage/rent, utilities, insurance)
- Any other expense categories relevant to your trade or industry

### Payroll (if you have employees)
- Payroll records and summaries for the year
- **941** quarterly payroll tax returns (or copies from your payroll provider)
- **940** annual federal unemployment tax return

### Contractor Payments
- Records of payments made to any individual contractor you paid $600 or more during the year
- Names, addresses, and SSNs/EINs for those contractors (needed to issue **1099-NEC** forms)

### Asset Purchases
- Invoices or receipts for equipment, vehicles, or other significant assets purchased during the year
- Trade-in or sale records for assets disposed of

## Prior-Year Tax Return

If this is your first time working with us, bring a copy of last year's federal (and state) tax return if you have it. This helps us verify carryover amounts, confirm your filing status history, and catch anything that might need to carry forward.

---

## Banking Information for Direct Deposit

If you're expecting a refund and want it deposited directly to your bank account:

- Bank name
- Routing number
- Account number

You can usually find both numbers on a check or through your bank's mobile app.

---

## Life Changes to Mention

Even if you don't have a document for every item, let us know if any of the following happened during the year. These events often affect your filing.

- **Marriage or divorce** — your filing status and withholding may need to change
- **New child or dependent** — birth, adoption, or a family member you supported
- **Home purchase or sale**
- **Job change or new side income**
- **Move between Georgia and Alabama** — you may have multi-state filing obligations
- **Retirement or first Social Security benefit**
- **Inheritance or receipt of a large gift**
- **Starting or closing a business**

---

## A Few Final Tips

- **Don't wait for everything to be perfect.** If you're missing a document, bring what you have and we can work around it.
- **Organize by category if you can.** Even a simple folder with labeled sections saves time.
- **When in doubt, bring it.** It's easier to set something aside than to realize you need it after you've left.
